Preparing a simple breakfast.

Precondition: there is edible food available, the stove or appliance works,
and the person is not so fatigued that operating heat safely is unreliable.

Steps: check what food is available and whether it looks and smells normal.
Wash hands before handling food. Choose a simple recipe matched to the
ingredients on hand, such as eggs and toast rather than something requiring
missing items. Gather the pan, spatula, plate, and any needed ingredients
before turning on heat, so nothing is left unattended while searching for a
tool. Set the burner to a moderate heat rather than the highest setting, to
reduce the chance of burning food or the pan. Watch the food while it cooks,
adjusting heat if it starts browning too quickly. When finished, turn the
burner off before plating the food, not after. Eat at a reasonable pace.
Afterward, let the pan cool if needed, then wash the pan, plate, and utensils,
and wipe the counter.

Success condition: the food is cooked through, the appliance is off, and the
cooking area is clean afterward.

Failure cases and corrections: if the pan starts smoking heavily, turn off the
heat immediately and move the pan off the burner if it is safe to do so; do
not add water to a grease fire. If food smells spoiled before cooking, discard
it rather than cooking it anyway. If uncertain whether a food is still safe,
check the date, look for visible mold or discoloration, and when still
uncertain, discard it rather than risk illness.

---

Food storage.

Cooked leftovers should be moved to a sealed container and refrigerated
within about two hours of cooking, sooner if the room is warm. Raw meat
should be stored separately from other food, ideally on a lower shelf, so
juices cannot drip onto items that will not be cooked further. Dry goods like
rice and pasta belong in sealed containers to avoid pests and moisture.
Perishable items should generally be used before their listed date; if a date
has passed, inspect smell, texture, and appearance before deciding whether to
use or discard the item, and when in doubt, discard it.

Precondition: refrigerator and containers are available and functioning.
Success condition: food is stored at a safe temperature, separated
appropriately, and sealed to reduce spoilage and contamination.

---

Washing dishes.

Steps: scrape food scraps into a bin or compost. Rinse dishes to remove loose
debris. Wash with soap and warm water, starting with the least soiled items,
such as glasses, and finishing with the most soiled, such as pans used for
frying. Rinse soap off thoroughly. Dry with a clean towel or place in a rack
to air dry. Wipe down the sink and counter afterward.

Failure case: if a pan has burnt-on food that does not come off with normal
washing, soak it in warm soapy water for a while rather than scrubbing hard
enough to damage the surface or injure a hand. If a glass breaks while
washing, stop, do not reach into the sink with bare hands, drain the water if
needed, and remove the pieces carefully or use a tool, then check for smaller
fragments before continuing.
